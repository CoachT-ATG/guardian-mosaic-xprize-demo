# Compliance Boundary

## Objective

Stay eligible for the XPRIZE while exposing the minimum possible amount of information and spending the minimum possible amount of money.

## Cost Policy

- Use existing Google Workspace subscriptions and existing owned Google data first.
- Do not add new paid tools unless they are required for the minimal judged demo path.
- Keep the Google Cloud and Gemini footprint to one minimal deployed proof path.
- Treat budgets and alerts as guardrails, not as permission to expand scope.

## Data Policy

- Use only data you already own or control.
- Import from Google Takeout only for your own accounts and only for the portions needed for the submission.
- Redact names, customer details, private correspondence, and any sensitive business records from public artifacts.
- Keep private Drive folders, raw imports, and source archives out of the public repo.

## GitHub Policy

- Public repo contains only contest-required source, sanitized prompts, demo assets, logs, screenshots, and docs.
- Do not publish proprietary methods, model weights, internal prompts, trade secrets, or patent-sensitive implementation details.
- Do not publish credentials, API keys, or internal URLs.

## Submission Policy

- Make the demo usable by judges.
- Keep the public narrative focused on the wellness-safe `ATG Responder Coherence Operator`.
- Include only the minimum evidence needed to show: working product, revenue, users, logs, API usage, and dashboard screenshots.
- Keep any future SaMD or PCCP versioning in a separate private track, not the public submission.

## Safety Policy

- No diagnosis.
- No treatment claims.
- No PHI in the public demo.
- No fitness-for-duty or return-to-duty outputs.
- No sensitive private data in GitHub.

## Operating Rule

If a piece of information is not needed to satisfy the rules or to demonstrate the product, do not publish it.
