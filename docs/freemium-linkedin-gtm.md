# Freemium LinkedIn GTM Plan

This document turns the LinkedIn / iOS freemium idea into a judge-safe operating plan.

## Core Thesis

Use LinkedIn as the fastest low-cost distribution channel to seed:

- early consumer beta users
- pilot decision-makers
- credibility signals from the responder community

Use the iOS freemium surface as a product-adoption wedge, not as the sole XPRIZE evidence path.

## Complexity Loop Analysis

### 1. Ideation Monte Carlo

Candidate paths considered:

- consumer-only iOS freemium
- agency-only pilot sales
- hybrid consumer + agency path
- app-first App Store launch
- LinkedIn-led beta with pilot conversion

### 2. RO Learning

Scoring criteria:

- revenue speed
- user evidence speed
- contest compliance
- scope risk
- operational cost
- likelihood of reviewer approval

### 3. QNPA Hybrid Rounds

Best bounded variant:

- use LinkedIn to recruit a small, high-signal beta cohort
- keep the app wellness-only and iOS-first
- convert the most engaged users into testimonials and referrals
- use those proofs to close agency pilots

### 4. Doc Strange Adversarial Analysis

Adversarial risks:

- trying to reach 100k subscribers before the XPRIZE window closes
- overbuilding consumer features before revenue exists
- leaking private Mosaic/IP logic into public marketing
- treating consumer virality as a substitute for contract revenue
- letting app-store launch delays block the actual pilot business

## Recommended Improvement

The original idea is too broad if it tries to do everything at once.

The better plan is:

1. Launch a 14-day iOS freemium beta for the wellness wedge.
2. Use LinkedIn to recruit responders, veterans, and public-safety-adjacent testers.
3. Route the strongest responses into paid pilot conversations.
4. Use agency pilots as the revenue engine for the XPRIZE evidence packet.
5. Keep consumer expansion as brand lift and later scale, not the only win condition.

## What The Freemium App Should Prove

- daily check-ins can be completed in under 60 seconds
- Flow / coherence prompts are useful
- users return without friction
- the app produces helpful aggregate trends
- human review remains the safety backstop
- the product is easy to explain to agency buyers

## What The Freemium App Should Not Try To Prove

- diagnosis
- treatment efficacy
- clinical claims
- fitness-for-duty
- return-to-duty
- PHI handling
- full consumer monetization before the pilot path exists

## Practical Conversion Ladder

LinkedIn post -> iOS beta signup -> 14-day activation -> testimonial / review -> paid pilot referral -> agency pilot -> XPRIZE evidence.

## Metrics To Track

- profile views
- beta signups
- activation rate
- day-2 and day-7 retention
- completed check-ins
- testimonial count
- pilot introductions
- paid pilot close rate

## 60-Day Reality Check

Targeting hundreds of thousands of paid subscribers inside 60 days is not a sound execution assumption for this contest window.

Treat scale as upside. Treat the XPRIZE requirement as:

- real users
- real revenue
- real evidence
- real deployment

The winning move is a smaller, defensible wedge that produces actual receipts fast.

## Google / Workspace Execution

Use existing subscriptions first:

- Drive for assets
- Docs for scripts and offers
- Sheets for lead tracking and revenue
- Forms for beta signup and feedback
- AppSheet for the pilot dashboard
- Gemini only for the minimal deployed proof path

## External Posting Rule

LinkedIn posts should be prepared as drafts first.

Do not publish until the content is reviewed for:

- no proprietary IP leakage
- no clinical overclaiming
- no PHI
- no security-sensitive detail
