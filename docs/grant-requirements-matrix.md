# Grant Requirements Matrix

This is the current working map for the public wellness MVP and pilot funding path.
Re-check every solicitation before submission, because deadlines and eligibility move.

## Rule 1: The MVP must satisfy the wellness lane first

The public demo and pilot must stay in the general-wellness lane:
- no diagnosis
- no treatment claims
- no PHI in the public repo
- no fitness-for-duty or return-to-duty outputs
- no clinical recommendations without separate governed review

The MVP should still exceed the common grant expectations by offering:
- peer support routing
- daily resilience check-ins
- short recovery prompts
- family-friendly resource pathways
- training and education modules
- agency-level aggregate reporting
- privacy-preserving evidence logs

## Federal Programs

### COPS LEMHWA

Official page: `https://cops.usdoj.gov/lemhwa`

Current posted requirements:
- supports delivery of mental health and wellness services for law enforcement officers and their families
- uses peer support, training, family resources, suicide prevention, stress reduction, clinical support, and other promising practices
- implementation projects target state, local, tribal, and territorial law enforcement agencies
- state and local governmental entities must comply with 8 U.S.C. §1373
- current page notes the FY25 solicitation is closed, so the page is for requirement mapping and historical structure, not current submission timing

How ATG exceeds it:
- daily check-ins + resilience briefs
- sponsor dashboard with aggregate-only metrics
- human review queue for distress flags
- family/resource routing without PHI exposure
- a pilot report that documents participation and trends

### BJA VALOR

Official page: `https://bja.ojp.gov/program/valor/overview`

Current posted requirements and themes:
- dedicated to officer safety and wellness
- supports training, resources, customized assistance, and practical resilience strategies
- law enforcement focus, agency and officer usability, and community safety linkage
- current funding page exists, but posted deadlines must be checked each cycle

How ATG exceeds it:
- daily flow-state support and transition rituals
- shift-aware guidance
- sponsor-ready aggregate reporting
- explicit privacy boundary and human accountability gate

### BJA JMHCP

Official page: `https://www.bja.ojp.gov/program/jmhcp/overview`

Current posted requirements and themes:
- collaborative project between criminal justice and mental health partners
- eligible applicants include states, local governments, Indian tribes, and tribal organizations
- plan, implement, or expand a justice and mental health collaboration program
- emphasizes early intervention, diversion opportunities, cross-training, and support services

How ATG exceeds it:
- human review queue and escalation routing
- agency/behavioral-health referral touchpoints
- workflow that can support cross-system collaboration without exposing PHI

### NIJ Safety, Health, and Wellness

Official page: `https://nij.ojp.gov/safety-health-and-wellness-research-portfolio`

What NIJ expects:
- research and evaluation, not just product claims
- safety, health, wellness, and stress risk framing
- measurable outcomes and data discipline
- often better suited for validation, benchmarking, and evidence generation than direct operational deployment

How ATG exceeds it:
- built-in evidence export
- pilot metrics, cohort tracking, and controlled sample outputs
- research-friendly logs with privacy boundaries

## State / Local Grants

State and local grants vary by jurisdiction, but common requirements usually include:
- agency executive sponsor
- local budget or match language if required
- privacy and data handling policy
- implementation schedule
- training plan
- sustainability plan
- measurable outcomes
- vendor or partner documentation

ATG should be prepared to exceed those requirements with:
- a one-page pilot offer
- a short implementation plan
- a privacy boundary statement
- sponsor dashboard screenshots
- user metrics and testimonials
- a final report template

## Winning Constraint

If a feature cannot be explained to an agency wellness lead, a grant reviewer, and a judge without exposing proprietary internals, it should stay private until a separate governed track exists.
